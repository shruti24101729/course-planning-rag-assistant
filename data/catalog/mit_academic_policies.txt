SECTION A: GRADING POLICIES
----------------------------

A.1 Grading System
MIT uses a 5.0-point grading scale:
  A (5.0) – Exceptional; outstanding work
  B (4.0) – Good; above average performance
  C (3.0) – Satisfactory; meets basic requirements
  D (2.0) – Passing; barely acceptable
  F (1.0) – Failing; subject must be repeated to receive credit

A.2 Pass/Fail Option
- Undergraduate students may elect to take any non-required subject on a Pass/Fail (P/F) basis.
- Required departmental subjects (Foundations and Headers) CANNOT be taken P/F.
- Advanced Undergraduate Subjects (AUS) CANNOT be taken P/F.
- Unrestricted electives and GIRs (with departmental approval) may be P/F.
- Students must declare P/F election before the end of the 5th week of the term.
- P/F elections are irrevocable after the declaration deadline.
- A "Pass" requires a grade of D or above; a D- does not constitute a pass at MIT.

A.3 Freshman Grading
- All subjects taken in the freshman fall semester are graded Pass/No-Record (P/NR).
  A grade of A, B, C, or D is recorded as "P" (Pass); F is recorded as "NR" (No Record).
- Starting in the freshman spring semester, letter grades appear on the transcript.
- P/NR grades do not count toward GPA.

A.4 Incomplete Grades
- A grade of "I" (Incomplete) may be assigned when a student cannot complete coursework 
  due to documented extenuating circumstances.
- Incomplete grades must be resolved within one term (Fall → resolved by end of Spring; 
  Spring → resolved by end of Fall).
- An unresolved Incomplete converts to a grade of "F" after the resolution deadline.
- Students with an outstanding Incomplete may not enroll in the next term's subject 
  that requires the incomplete subject as a prerequisite until the Incomplete is resolved.

A.5 Grade Changes
- Faculty may submit grade changes within one academic year of the original grade posting.
- Grade changes for reasons other than clerical error require departmental dean approval.

SECTION B: REGISTRATION AND ENROLLMENT
---------------------------------------

B.1 Credit Limits
- Freshmen (fall): maximum 54 units per semester
- Freshmen (spring): maximum 54 units per semester
- Sophomores, Juniors, Seniors: maximum 57 units per semester (standard)
- Overload petition: students above 4th semester may petition for up to 66 units; 
  overloads require academic advisor sign-off and dean approval
- Minimum enrollment for aid eligibility: 36 units per semester

B.2 Add/Drop Deadlines
- Add deadline: end of Week 2 of the semester
- Drop deadline (without W on transcript): end of Week 5 of the semester  
- Late drop deadline (W appears on transcript): end of Week 10 of the semester
- Subjects dropped after Week 10 require a petition and dean approval; 
  a "W" (withdrawal) appears on the transcript

B.3 Enrollment in Prerequisites
- Students must have completed all listed prerequisites before enrolling in a subject.
- Concurrent enrollment (co-requisite) is permitted only where explicitly noted in the 
  course catalog entry (e.g., 6.006 allows concurrent enrollment in 6.042J).
- The Registrar enforces prerequisite checking at enrollment; students who do not meet 
  prerequisites must obtain written instructor permission to override the prerequisite block.
- Instructor permission to enroll does NOT waive the academic requirement—if a student 
  earns a failing grade in a prerequisite subject they never completed, the subsequent 
  subject credit may be withheld pending review.

B.4 Cross-Registration
- MIT students may cross-register at Harvard University for subjects not offered at MIT.
- Cross-registered subjects must be approved by the student's academic advisor and the 
  MIT Registrar.
- Credit awarded for cross-registered subjects counts as transfer credit (see Section D).

SECTION C: REPETITION OF SUBJECTS
------------------------------------

C.1 General Policy
- Students may repeat any subject for which they received a D or F.
- Both the original grade and the repeat grade appear on the official transcript.
- The higher of the two grades counts toward satisfying prerequisite requirements.
- Both grades are included in GPA calculation (no grade replacement).
- A subject may be repeated at most twice (original attempt + 2 retakes); 
  a third attempt requires petition to the EECS Undergraduate Office.

C.2 Impact on Financial Aid
- Repeated subjects where a passing grade (C or above) was previously earned 
  may not count toward minimum enrollment for financial aid purposes.

SECTION D: TRANSFER CREDIT
-----------------------------

D.1 Advanced Placement (AP) Credit
- AP Calculus BC (score 5): satisfies 18.01 (Calculus I)
- AP Chemistry (score 5): satisfies Chemistry GIR (3.091 equivalent)
- AP Physics C: Mechanics (score 5): satisfies Physics I GIR (8.01 equivalent)
- AP Computer Science A (score 5): may earn up to 6 units of unrestricted elective 
  credit; does NOT satisfy 6.009 or any Foundation requirement
- AP credit is awarded after submission of official AP score reports to the MIT Registrar

D.2 Transfer Subjects
- Transfer subjects from accredited institutions may be approved for MIT credit.
- Maximum 60 units of transfer credit may count toward the 180-unit graduation requirement.
- Foundation Subjects (6.009, 6.042J, 6.004, 6.006) cannot be satisfied by transfer 
  credit without written approval from the EECS Undergraduate Committee.
- Transfer credit requests must be submitted with a course syllabus and instructor 
  contact for verification; processing takes 4–8 weeks.

D.3 International Transfer Credit
- Credit from non-US institutions is evaluated on a case-by-case basis by the 
  MIT International Students Office in consultation with the relevant department.

SECTION E: WITHDRAWAL AND LEAVE OF ABSENCE
--------------------------------------------

E.1 Medical Leave
- Students may take a medical leave of absence with documentation from MIT Medical.
- Credit taken during the leave term is voided.
- Subjects in progress at time of leave receive a grade of "W" (withdrawn).

E.2 Personal Leave
- Students may take up to two semesters of personal leave.
- Leave must be declared before the end of Week 10 of the semester.
- Students on leave must apply for reinstatement at least 6 weeks before their 
  intended return term.

E.3 Academic Withdrawal
- Students with a cumulative GPA below 2.0 after two consecutive semesters may be 
  subject to academic withdrawal.
- Academic withdrawal is processed by the Committee on Academic Performance (CAP).

SECTION F: PREREQUISITE EXCEPTIONS AND INSTRUCTOR CONSENT
-----------------------------------------------------------

F.1 Instructor Consent Override
- An instructor may grant consent to waive a specific prerequisite.
- Consent must be documented in writing (email to registrar@mit.edu cc'd to the student).
- Instructor consent does NOT waive ALL prerequisites; it applies only to the 
  specific prerequisite listed in the consent document.
- Instructor consent may not be used to enroll in a subject where the student 
  has an outstanding F in the prerequisite that has not been retaken.

F.2 Prerequisite Substitution
- A student may petition the EECS Undergraduate Office to substitute an equivalent 
  subject for a listed prerequisite (e.g., a graduate-level stat course in lieu of 18.650).
- Substitution petitions require: (a) course syllabi for both subjects, (b) advisor 
  signature, (c) instructor signature for the target subject.

F.3 Concurrent Prerequisites (Co-requisites)
- When a subject lists a co-requisite, the co-requisite may be:
  (a) taken concurrently in the same term, OR
  (b) completed in a previous term (satisfies the co-requisite by prior completion)
- A co-requisite may NOT be deferred to a future term after taking the subject; 
  the student must take it either before or at the same time.

SECTION G: ACADEMIC INTEGRITY
-------------------------------

G.1 Honor Code
- All work submitted must be the student's own unless explicitly authorized for collaboration.
- Violations of the MIT Academic Integrity policy may result in subject failure, 
  suspension, or expulsion.

G.2 Collaboration Policy
- Each subject sets its own collaboration policy; students must read and follow 
  the subject's stated policy.
- When in doubt, students should consult the instructor before collaborating.

SECTION H: ADVISING AND PLANNING RESOURCES
--------------------------------------------

H.1 Academic Advisors
- Each EECS undergraduate is assigned a faculty advisor who must approve term course plans.
- Advisor approval is required for: overloads, late drops, leave of absence, 
  and prerequisite petitions.

H.2 EECS Undergraduate Office
- Location: 38-476 (Building 38, Room 476)
- Email: ug-eecs@mit.edu
- Office hours: Monday–Friday, 9am–5pm ET

H.3 Schedule of Classes
- MIT's official schedule of classes (which lists actual section times and availability) 
  is published at student.mit.edu/cgi-bin/sfprwsch.sh
- Course catalog entries list which terms a subject is "typically offered" but do NOT 
  guarantee offering in any specific year; always verify using the Schedule of Classes.

H.4 Degree Audit
- Students may view their degree audit (completed requirements vs. remaining requirements) 
  via the MIT WebSIS portal at: websis.mit.edu
- The degree audit is updated within 2 weeks after each term's grades are posted.
