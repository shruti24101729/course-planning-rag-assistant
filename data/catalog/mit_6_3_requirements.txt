COURSE 6-3: COMPUTER SCIENCE AND ENGINEERING
Bachelor of Science Degree Requirements
==============================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The 6-3 program (Computer Science and Engineering) requires 180–198 units of credit, 
distributed among General Institute Requirements (GIRs), Departmental Program requirements, 
and electives. Students must maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.5 to remain in good 
standing in the major.

==============================================================================
SECTION 1: GENERAL INSTITUTE REQUIREMENTS (GIRs)
==============================================================================

All MIT undergraduates must complete the GIRs, which include:

Science Core (6 subjects)
  - Chemistry (GIR): 3.091 or 5.111 or 5.112
  - Physics I (GIR): 8.01 or 8.01L or 8.012 or 8.01T
  - Physics II (GIR): 8.02 or 8.02T or 8.022
  - Biology (GIR): 7.012 or 7.013 or 7.014 or 7.015 or 7.016
  - Calculus I (GIR): 18.01 or 18.01A
  - Calculus II (GIR): 18.02 or 18.02A

Laboratory (1 subject):
  - Any GIR Lab subject

HASS (Humanities, Arts, Social Sciences):
  - 8 HASS subjects required (at least 2 in each of HASS-A, HASS-H, HASS-S)
  - At least 2 subjects in a concentration

Communication Requirement:
  - Phase I (CI-H): 2 Communication-Intensive subjects in HASS
  - Phase II (CI-M): 1 Communication-Intensive subject in major (6.033 satisfies this)

Physical Education (PE):
  - 4 PE credits required

==============================================================================
SECTION 2: DEPARTMENTAL PROGRAM (REQUIRED SUBJECTS)
==============================================================================

Foundation Subjects (all required):
  [F1] 6.009 – Fundamentals of Programming (12 units)
  [F2] 6.042J – Mathematics for Computer Science (12 units)
  [F3] 6.004 – Computation Structures (12 units)
  [F4] 6.006 – Introduction to Algorithms (12 units)

Header Subjects (all required):
  [H1] 6.031 – Software Construction (15 units)
  [H2] 6.033 – Computer Systems Engineering (12 units) [also satisfies CI-M]
  [H3] 6.036 – Introduction to Machine Learning (12 units)
  [H4] 6.046J – Design and Analysis of Algorithms (12 units)

Advanced Undergraduate Subjects (AUS) - 3 required:
  Students must complete at least THREE (3) AUS subjects from the approved list.
  At least one AUS must be from the Systems AUS list and at least one from the 
  Theory/AI AUS list.

  Systems AUS (choose at least 1):
    - 6.033 (already required as header, does NOT double-count)
    - 6.814 – Database Systems
    - 6.824 – Distributed Systems
    - 6.829 – Computer Networks
    - 6.858 – Computer Systems Security
    - 6.035 – Computer Language Engineering

  Theory/AI AUS (choose at least 1):
    - 6.854J – Advanced Algorithms
    - 6.867 – Machine Learning (Graduate)
    - 6.034 – Artificial Intelligence

  Either category:
    - 6.UAP – Undergraduate Advanced Project (with faculty approval)

Lab AUS (optional but recommended):
    - 6.UAT – Oral Communication (satisfies CI-M requirement if 6.033 not taken; 
      cannot double-count with CI-M)

TOTAL AUS REQUIRED: 3 subjects minimum (at least 1 Systems + at least 1 Theory/AI)

==============================================================================
SECTION 3: UNRESTRICTED ELECTIVES
==============================================================================

Students must complete sufficient subjects to bring total units to 180 minimum.
Unrestricted electives may be any MIT subjects or approved transfer subjects.
Maximum 12 units of physical education may count toward electives.

==============================================================================
SECTION 4: CREDIT AND UNIT REQUIREMENTS
==============================================================================

- Minimum total: 180 units for graduation
- Maximum load per term: 57 units (freshman year); 66 units (upper years)
  Exception: students may petition for overloads above 57/66 units
- Minimum passing grade: D (1.0 on 5.0 scale)
- Subjects graded A-F; pass/fail available for electives only
- To receive credit for a subject that was previously failed, student must retake
  and pass; both grades appear on transcript

Residency Requirement:
- Students must complete at least 4 semesters in residence at MIT
- At least 3 of the 4 required AUS/Header subjects in the final year must be 
  taken at MIT (transfer credit for these is limited)

==============================================================================
SECTION 5: GPA AND STANDING REQUIREMENTS
==============================================================================

Good Standing:
- Cumulative GPA of 3.5 or above (on 5.0 scale) is required for good standing in 6-3
- Students below 3.5 are placed on departmental warning and must meet with advisor
- Students who remain below 3.5 for two consecutive terms may be asked to transfer 
  to a different major

Grade Scale (MIT 5.0 system):
  A (Exceptional) = 5.0
  B (Good)        = 4.0
  C (Satisfactory)= 3.0
  D (Passing)     = 2.0
  F (Failing)     = 1.0

Prerequisite Grade Requirement:
- No explicit minimum grade required to satisfy a prerequisite unless stated 
  in the course catalog entry. A passing grade (D or above) normally satisfies 
  a prerequisite.
- However, some instructors may require a grade of C or above; this is noted 
  in the individual course description or course policy document.

==============================================================================
SECTION 6: TRANSFER CREDIT AND ADVANCED STANDING
==============================================================================

- AP credit: Calculus I (18.01) can be satisfied by AP Calculus BC score of 5
- Transfer credit for MIT subjects: requires petition to the EECS Undergraduate 
  Office; committee reviews equivalency
- A maximum of 60 units of transfer credit may count toward the 180-unit minimum
- Transfer subjects may NOT be used to satisfy Foundation Subjects [F1]–[F4] 
  without explicit approval from the EECS Undergraduate Committee

==============================================================================
SECTION 7: DOUBLE-COUNTING RULES
==============================================================================

- No subject may simultaneously satisfy both a GIR and a departmental requirement
- CI-M credit: 6.033 satisfies the CI-M requirement; no other departmental 
  required subject satisfies CI-M
- An AUS subject may NOT also be used to satisfy a header requirement
- 6.UAP may satisfy one AUS slot only with written permission from both the 
  supervising faculty member and the EECS Undergraduate Office

==============================================================================
SECTION 8: ACADEMIC POLICIES SPECIFIC TO 6-3
==============================================================================

Freshmen:
- Freshmen are graded Pass/No Record (P/NR) in the fall semester; grades appear 
  from the spring semester onward
- Freshmen may not enroll in 6.004 or 6.006 without completion of 6.009
- Freshmen may not enroll in more than 54 units in the spring semester

Enrollment in Graduate Subjects:
- Undergraduates may enroll in 6.800-level (graduate) subjects with permission 
  of the instructor; instructor permission does not waive listed prerequisites
- Undergraduates must still complete all listed prerequisites for graduate subjects

Repeated Subjects:
- A subject may be repeated at most once without special petition
- Both grades appear on transcript and both count toward GPA
- If retaken for a better grade, only the higher grade counts toward prerequisite 
  eligibility (but both remain on transcript)
