==============================================================================
6.009 / 6.009 Fundamentals of Programming
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-4-4)
Prerequisites: None
Description: Introduction to programming and computational problem solving using Python. Topics include computation models, data types, functions, recursion, algorithms, object-oriented programming, and software engineering principles. Weekly lab sessions provide hands-on programming experience.
Offered: Fall, Spring, Summer
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.042J / 18.062J Mathematics for Computer Science
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-4-4)
Prerequisites: Calculus I (18.01 or equivalent)
Description: Elementary discrete mathematics for science and engineering. Topics include formal logic notation, proof methods, induction, sets, relations, graph theory, integer arithmetic, probability, and counting. Emphasizes mathematical reasoning and applying tools of discrete math to computer science problems.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.004 Computation Structures
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-4-4)
Prerequisites: 6.009
Description: Fundamentals of digital design and computer architecture. Covers combinational and sequential logic, finite state machines, instruction set architectures, pipelining, caching, and memory hierarchies. Weekly labs use simulation tools to design circuits and processors.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.006 Introduction to Algorithms
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-4-4)
Prerequisites: 6.009; 6.042J or concurrent enrollment in 6.042J
Description: Introduction to mathematical modeling of computational problems, as well as common algorithms, algorithmic paradigms, and data structures used to solve them. Topics include sorting, hash tables, searching, dynamic programming, graph algorithms, and shortest paths. Emphasis on algorithm design and analysis.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units
Note: Students may take 6.042J concurrently as a co-requisite.

==============================================================================
6.034 Artificial Intelligence
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-4-4)
Prerequisites: 6.009
Description: Introduction to artificial intelligence. Topics include problem solving (search), knowledge representation, inference, planning, machine learning, neural networks, probabilistic reasoning, and natural language processing. Weekly lab assignments in Python.
Offered: Fall
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.036 Introduction to Machine Learning
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-4-4)
Prerequisites: 6.006; 18.06 or 18.C06J; 18.650 or 6.041B or permission of instructor
Description: Principles, algorithms, and applications of machine learning from the point of view of modeling and prediction. Topics include linear models, classification, neural networks, deep learning, generalization, and unsupervised learning. Weekly programming labs.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.046J Design and Analysis of Algorithms
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-4-4)
Prerequisites: 6.006
Description: Techniques for the design and analysis of efficient algorithms, emphasizing methods useful in practice. Topics include amortized analysis, randomization, fingerprinting, word-level parallelism, bit scaling, dynamic programming, network flow, computational geometry, number-theoretic algorithms, polynomial and matrix calculations, caching, and parallel computing.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.031 Software Construction
==============================================================================
Units: 15 (4-6-5)
Prerequisites: 6.009
Description: Principles of software construction: safety from bugs, ease of understanding, and readiness for change. Topics include specifications, unit testing, abstract data types, design patterns, concurrency, and version control. Extensive programming in Java.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 15 units

==============================================================================
6.033 Computer Systems Engineering
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (3-3-6)
Prerequisites: 6.004; 6.009
Description: Topics on the engineering of computer software and hardware systems: techniques for controlling complexity; strong modularity using client-server design, virtual memory, and threads; networks; atomicity and coordination of parallel activities; security, and fault tolerance. Substantial writing component.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units
CI-M (Communication Intensive in Major) Subject

==============================================================================
6.814 Database Systems
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (3-3-6)
Prerequisites: 6.033
Description: Topics include design of database systems, the relational model, relational algebra, SQL, schema normalization, transaction management, concurrency control, crash recovery, B-trees, hash indexing, and query optimization. Students complete a semester-long project to implement a database system.
Offered: Fall
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.824 Distributed Systems
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (3-3-6)
Prerequisites: 6.033; 6.004
Description: Principles of distributed computer systems. Topics include fault tolerance, replication, consistency models, distributed transactions, consensus algorithms (Paxos, Raft), map-reduce, Spanner, and case studies of real systems. Labs involve building distributed systems in Go.
Offered: Spring
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.830 Database Systems (Graduate)
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (3-3-6)
Prerequisites: 6.814 or permission of instructor
Description: Graduate-level topics in database systems including query processing, query optimization, storage systems, parallel and distributed databases, column stores, and modern hardware considerations.
Offered: Fall
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.035 Computer Language Engineering
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (3-3-6)
Prerequisites: 6.004; 6.031
Description: Analyzes issues associated with the implementation of higher-level programming languages. Topics include syntax analysis, handling of user-defined types and type-checking, context-sensitive analysis, code generation, and optimization. Students build a compiler as a semester-long project.
Offered: Fall
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.041 Probabilistic Systems Analysis
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-4-4)
Prerequisites: 18.02; 6.042J or concurrent enrollment
Description: Introduction to probability and applications. Topics include probability spaces, random variables, expectations, transforms, Bernoulli and Poisson processes, finite Markov chains, and limit theorems.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.867 Machine Learning (Graduate)
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (3-3-6)
Prerequisites: 6.036; 18.06; 18.650 or 6.041
Description: Graduate-level machine learning covering statistical learning theory, kernel methods, support vector machines, graphical models, inference, and deep learning from a theoretical perspective.
Offered: Fall
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.854J Advanced Algorithms
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (3-3-6)
Prerequisites: 6.046J
Description: Advanced topics in the design and analysis of algorithms: amortized data structures, network flow algorithms, linear programming, randomized algorithms, online algorithms, approximation algorithms, and computational geometry.
Offered: Fall
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.858 Computer Systems Security
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (3-3-6)
Prerequisites: 6.033
Description: Design and implementation of secure computer systems. Topics include threat models, attacks, and defenses at system, network, and application layers. Topics include buffer overflows, sandboxing, web security, network security, cryptography, and anonymous communication.
Offered: Fall
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.829 Computer Networks
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (3-3-6)
Prerequisites: 6.033
Description: Topics include routing, congestion control, software-defined networking, data center networks, network measurement, and wireless networks. Students complete labs involving implementation and analysis of networking protocols.
Offered: Fall
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
6.UAT Oral Communication (CI-M)
==============================================================================
Units: 6 (2-0-4)
Prerequisites: Permission of instructor; enrollment restricted to 6-3 students in their senior year
Description: Provides practice in oral communication skills for computer scientists. Topics include technical presentations, interview skills, and professional communication. Fulfills CI-M requirement for Course 6-3.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 6 units

==============================================================================
6.UAP Undergraduate Advanced Project
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (0-4-8)
Prerequisites: Senior standing in Course 6; minimum GPA of 4.0; permission of supervising faculty
Description: Independent research project supervised by a faculty member. Students complete a substantial project in computer science and present their work in written and oral form.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
18.01 Calculus I (Single Variable)
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (5-0-7)
Prerequisites: None (placement exam recommended)
Description: Differentiation and integration of functions of one variable, with applications. Topics include limits, derivatives, chain rule, implicit differentiation, related rates, max/min, definite and indefinite integrals, fundamental theorem of calculus.
Offered: Fall, Spring, Summer
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
18.02 Calculus II (Multivariable)
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (5-0-7)
Prerequisites: 18.01
Description: Calculus of several variables. Topics include vectors and vector-valued functions, partial derivatives, double and triple integrals, line integrals, surface integrals, Green's theorem, Stokes' theorem, and the divergence theorem.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
18.06 Linear Algebra
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-0-8)
Prerequisites: 18.02
Description: Basic subject on matrix theory and linear algebra, emphasizing topics useful in other disciplines. Topics include systems of equations, vector spaces, matrices and linear transformations, determinants, eigenvalues, and positive definite matrices.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
18.650 Statistics for Applications
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-0-8)
Prerequisites: 18.02; 6.041 or 18.600
Description: Topics in statistics with applications. Covers estimation, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, regression, and Bayesian methods.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units

==============================================================================
18.600 Probability and Random Variables
==============================================================================
Units: 12 (4-0-8)
Prerequisites: 18.02
Description: Probability spaces, random variables, distribution functions, densities, expectations, characteristic functions, central limit theorem, Markov chains.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Credit: 12 units
